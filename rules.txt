### Part 1: Every Single Linguistic Rule

**Phonological Rules (Pronunciation shifts in Cape Muslim Afrikaans):**
*   The standard Afrikaans diphthong **ui** [oey] is pronounced as **ei** [ei] (e.g., *uiwe* > *eiwe*).
*   The standard Afrikaans past tense prefix **ge-** [xə] is pronounced as **ga-** [xa].
*   A double **d** or **t** in the middle of a word is pronounced as **r** (e.g., *middag* becomes *marrag*; *middel* becomes *mirrel*; *bottel* becomes *borrel*).

**Morphological and Syntactical Rules:**
*   **Adverbs of degree:** Arabic noun + Afrikaans suffix **-ste** (e.g., *sahieh* + *ste* = *sahiehste*).
*   **Pluralization:** Arabic noun + Afrikaans suffix **-s** (e.g., *salaah* + *s* = *salaahs*; *hoekoem* + *s* = *hoekoems*).
*   **Abstract Nouns from Adjectives/Verbs:** Arabic/Malayo-Polynesian word + Afrikaans suffix **-heid** or **-skap** (e.g., *waadjieb* + *gheid* = *waadjiebgheid*; *soppang* + *heid* = *soppangheid*; *nabee* + *skap* = *nabeeskap*; *warrais* + *skap* = *warraiskap*).
*   **Nouns from Verbs:** Malayo-Polynesian verb + Afrikaans suffix **-er** (e.g., *slamblee* + *er* = *slamblie-er*).
*   **Past Tense Verbs:** Afrikaans prefix **ge-** + Arabic/Malayo-Polynesian verb (e.g., *ge* + *nieyat* = *genieyat*; *ge* + *salaah* = *gesalaah*; *ge* + *goedbah* = *gegoetbah*; *ge* + *talak* = *getalak*).
*   **Adjectives:** Malayo-Polynesian word + Afrikaans suffix **-se** (e.g., *djapandoelie* + *se* = *djapandoeliese*).
*   **Composite Syntactical Order:** When creating composites, the language uses the Afrikaans Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) / Adjective-Noun order rather than the Malayo-Polynesian/Arabic Noun-Adjective order (e.g., *ouwal-moereeds* instead of *moereeds-ouwal*; *mannie-kamer* instead of *kamer-mannie*).

**Orthographic and Transcription Rules (Writing Afrikaans in Arabic Script):**
*   **Short Vowels:** Short 'a' is indicated by *fatha*; short 'i' by *kasra*; short 'oe' (u) by *damma* + *waw* (or sometimes just *damma*); short 'o' by *fatha* + *damma*.
*   **Long Vowels:** Long 'aa' is indicated by *fatha* + *alif*; long 'ie' by *kasra* + *ya*. 
*   **Specific Afrikaans Vowels & Diphthongs:** 
    *   'e' (as in "met") = *fatha* + *kasra*.
    *   'eu' = *fatha* + *kasra* + *waw*.
    *   'ai' or 'y' = *fatha* + *ya*.
    *   'ei' = *fatha* + *kasra* + *ya*.
    *   'au' = *fatha* + *waw*.
    *   'aai' = *fatha* + *alif* + *ya*.
*   **Consonants Modified for Afrikaans:**
    *   'p' = **پ** (borrowed from Persian/Turkish).
    *   'g' (as in "great") = **گ** (Kaf with an upper line).
    *   'gh' (guttural Afrikaans g) = **غ** or **خ**.
    *   'ng' = **نگ** (Kaf with an upper line shifted left).
    *   'tj' = **چ**.
    *   'dj' = **ج**.
    *   'v' = represented by 'f' (**ف**).
    *   'w' = represented by **ۏ** or **و**.
    *   'z' = completely absent; not used in the texts.
    *   's' = normally **س**, but emphatic **ص** is used in specific words like *netsoes*.
*   **Gemination & Punctuation:** No Arabic *tashdid* (doubling of consonants) is used; repeated letters are simply written twice or avoided. Capital letters are absent, and sentence breaks are rarely indicated except by a horizontal dash or double colon (:). Words starting with a vowel take an *alif* as a carrier.

### 1. The Exhaustive Orthographic & Spelling Rules (Writing Afrikaans in Arabic Script)
To write Afrikaans, the writers had to invent a completely new orthographic system using the Arabic alphabet. Every single rule they used is as follows:

**Consonant Rules:**
*   **b, d, f** are represented by the Arabic letters *ba*, *dal*, and *fa*.
*   **g (guttural Afrikaans 'g')** is represented by *kha* (خ). This represents the laryngeal pronunciation used by Cape Muslims (e.g., *ghamaak*, *ghasien*).
*   **g (as in English 'great')** is represented by a modified *kaf* with an upper stroke (گ), borrowed from Persian/Turkish.
*   **ng** is represented by a *kaf* with an upper stroke shifted slightly to the left (نگ), similar to Persian.
*   **h** is represented by the Arabic *ha* (ه), not the laryngeal *ha* (ح).
*   **j (as in *baadjie*)** is represented by *jim* (ج).
*   **k** is represented by *kaf* (ك).
*   **l, m, n** are represented by *lam*, *mim*, and *nun*.
*   **p** is represented by *pe* (پ), a *ba* with three dots, borrowed from Persian/Turkish.
*   **r** is represented by *ra* (ر).
*   **s** is almost always represented by *sin* (س). The emphatic *sad* (ص) is uniquely reserved for the words *netsoes* and *soes*.
*   **t** is represented by *ta* (ت).
*   **tj (as in *djonkitjies*)** is represented by *che* (چ), a *jim* with three dots borrowed from Persian/Turkish.
*   **v** does not exist in the alphabet; it is always represented by *f* (*fa*).
*   **w** is usually represented by *waw* (و), though occasionally a labial *w* is distinguished from a dentilabial *w* by using *fa*.
*   **z** does not exist in these texts.
*   **Initial Vowels** must be preceded by an *alif* (ا) to act as a "carrier" for the vowel sound.

**Vowel and Diphthong Rules:**
*   **a (kort)** = *fatha*.
*   **aa (lang)** = *fatha* + *alif* (or a vertical stroke).
*   **e (as in 'met')** = *fatha* + *kasra*. Because this is a double marking, one sign is often accidentally omitted by writers.
*   **i (kort as in 'dit')** = *kasra*.
*   **ie (lang as in 'sien')** = *kasra* + *ya*.
*   **oe (as in 'moet')** = *damma* + *waw* (sometimes just a *damma* is used for both 'oe' and 'o').
*   **o (kort as in 'hom')** = *fatha* + *damma*.
*   **ai (as in 'tyd')** = *fatha* + *ya*.
*   **ei (as in 'skoonheid')** = *fatha* + *kasra* + *ya*.
*   **au (as in 'gou')** = *fatha* + *waw*.
*   **eu (as in 'volbring')** = *fatha* + *kasra* + *waw*.
*   **oei (as in 'hulle/hoeli')** = *waw* + *ya*.
*   **aai (as in 'kwaai')** = *fatha* + *alif* + *ya* accompanied by a *hamza*.

**Grammatical Markings and Punctuation Rules:**
*   **Sukun:** Used to show a vowelless consonant, though writers frequently omitted it by mistake.
*   **Tashdid (Consonant Doubling):** Never used. If a letter is doubled, the writers either wrote the letter twice in a row (e.g., *wanier*) or ignored the doubling altogether (e.g., *difiranta* instead of *different*).
*   **Tanwin and Ta'marbuta:** Arabic noun endings are completely discarded for Afrikaans words.
*   **Numerals:** Arabic numbers are never used; all numbers are written out as full words.
*   **Capitalization:** Does not exist.
*   **Punctuation:** A horizontal dash is used as a full stop. A double colon (:) is used to separate Arabic text from Afrikaans translation.
